Leading menswear brands in the 2020s

The 2020s menswear landscape is shaped by digital culture, hybrid lifestyles, and the tension between luxury experimentation and functional performance. This page highlights several influential brands whose aesthetics define contemporary style – from conceptual runway silhouettes to technical outdoor gear.

Across these brands we see oversized volumes, sharp tailoring, distressed surfaces, and high-performance fabrics. Together they reflect how menswear now moves fluidly between street, runway, and mountainside.

Balenciaga

Balenciaga pushes exaggeration and irony, turning everyday garments into hyper-scaled silhouettes that comment on celebrity culture and consumerism.

  • Silhouette: Extreme shoulders, oversized hoodies, long coats.
  • Materials: Heavy cotton fleece, coated denim, structured tailoring fabrics.
  • Signature look: Layered streetwear with luxury logos and distorted proportions.
  • Cultural role: Bridges meme culture, luxury fashion, and digital image-making.

Rick Owens

Rick Owens defines a dark, architectural vision of menswear that mixes draped softness with severe structure.

  • Silhouette: Longline tops, dropped crotch pants, stacked boots.
  • Materials: Washed leather, soft jerseys, textured wovens.
  • Signature look: Monochrome layering with asymmetric cuts and strong shoulders.
  • Cultural role: Cult brand for avant-garde, gender-fluid, subcultural fashion communities.

Dior Men

Dior Men refines classic Parisian tailoring, fusing couture techniques with contemporary street references and youth culture.

  • Silhouette: Sharp suits, slim trousers, structured outerwear.
  • Materials: Fine wool, mohair blends, embroidered and printed textiles.
  • Signature look: Tailored jackets styled with sneakers, knitwear, and casual accessories.
  • Cultural role: Connects luxury heritage with collaborations, idols, and global pop culture.

Arc'teryx

Arc'teryx represents the technical side of 2020s menswear, where performance outerwear becomes everyday uniform.

  • Silhouette: Streamlined shells, articulated sleeves, ergonomic seams.
  • Materials: Gore-Tex and other waterproof, breathable high-tech fabrics.
  • Signature look: Minimal jackets in saturated colors styled with cargos and trail shoes.
  • Cultural role: Core label in gorpcore and “outdoor city” aesthetics.
